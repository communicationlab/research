About the group

A research home for the next generation of connectivity.

The Communication Systems Research Group investigates how information moves across space — reliably, securely, and efficiently. Our work spans the full stack: from spectrum policy and dynamic sharing, through modulation, coding and estimation theory, up to machine-learning-driven radios and 6G network design.

We build and evaluate real systems — software-defined radios, mmWave and terahertz spectrum models, and channel/link simulators — and pair experiment with rigorous theory. Our members graduate fluent in both the mathematics of communication and the systems that make it physical.

We collaborate with academic and industry partners across telecom and beyond, and welcome motivated students at every level.

What we care about

Principles that guide every project in the group.

  • Spectrum is finite. Design for utilization, sharing, and reuse.
  • Theory meets systems. Every model earns its place in a real link.
  • Open by default. Reproducible code, data, and results.
  • People before papers. We train engineers, not just publish.
