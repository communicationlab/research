About the group

Communication that is faster, denser, and more intelligent.

The Communication Systems Research Group investigates how information travels — through radio spectrum, through light, and through learning algorithms that make networks decide for themselves.

Housed in the Department of Electrical & Electronic Engineering at BRAC University's BSRM School of Engineering, the group studies the full communication stack: from the physics of electromagnetic propagation to the machine-learning policies that allocate resources in real time.

Our work sits at two frontiers at once. On one side is spectrum — dynamic sharing between 4G, 5G, and Wi-Fi, cognitive-radio access, and reuse models reaching millimeter-wave and terahertz bands for 6G. On the other is intelligence — deep reinforcement learning that optimizes modulation, coding, and scheduling for ultra-reliable, low-latency links, including optical and vehicular systems. Together they define how future networks will carry more information with the same limited spectrum.

What we focus on

We deliberately span the electromagnetic spectrum, because tomorrow's networks will not live in a single band. The same design questions — capacity, reliability, efficiency, coexistence — recur from sub-6 GHz radio up to optical frequencies, and our research follows them across every band.

This breadth lets us treat radio, millimeter-wave, terahertz, and optical wireless as one continuous design space rather than isolated silos.

How we work

Principles behind the research.

01

Model, then measure

We build tractable analytical models of interference, capacity, and efficiency, then validate them against simulation and, where possible, testbeds.

02

Learn from the channel

Where closed-form design falls short, learning-based agents adapt directly to the radio environment to squeeze out reliability and efficiency.

03

Publish and share

Findings go out through IEEE and international venues, and into the courses we teach — communication, wireless, machine learning, and beyond.
